# State of the Art in RAG, Retrieval, Search & Deep Research (June 2026): A SOTA Corpus and Strategic Improvement Plan for OmniRAG

## TL;DR
- **The three retrieval paradigms are converging, and the winning architecture is a tri-modal router**: reasoning-based/vectorless (PageIndex-style tree navigation, 98.7% on FinanceBench), dense/multi-vector (ColPali + MUVERA + late chunking), and deterministic lexical (BM25/SPLADE-v3 over Tantivy). OmniRAG's instinct to combine all three is correct and validated by 2025-2026 research; the gap is in orchestration, evaluation rigor, and a handful of newer techniques (HippoRAG 2, contextual retrieval, agentic hierarchical retrieval interfaces, test-time multi-vector scaling).
- **OmniRAG can credibly out-position Pinecone/Weaviate/LlamaParse on cost and control** via a serverless Bring-Your-Own-Cloud (LanceDB-on-S3 + KùzuDB + Tantivy) architecture plus a one-time "heavy preprocessing" tier that amortizes expensive index-time compute (tree-building, contextual enrichment, multi-vector embedding, KV-cache pre-materialization) to make agentic query-time cheaper, faster, and more accurate.
- **The single biggest accuracy lever for the user's document types (financial filings, research papers, resumes) is structure-aware + contextual preprocessing**, not a better embedding model: Anthropic's Contextual Retrieval cut the top-20-chunk retrieval failure rate by **49% (5.7%→2.9%), and by 67% (→1.9%) when combined with reranking**; reasoning-based navigation of TOC trees; and visual-document retrieval (ColPali/ColQwen) that bypasses lossy parsing entirely.

## Key Findings

1. **Vectorless/reasoning RAG is real and SOTA on structured documents.** PageIndex (VectifyAI; Mingtian Zhang & Yu Tang, "PageIndex: Next-Generation Vectorless, Reasoning-based RAG," Sept 2025; MIT license, **32.5k GitHub stars / 2.8k forks** mid-2026) builds a TOC-style hierarchical tree and lets an LLM navigate it via tree search; its Mafin 2.5 system hit **98.7% on FinanceBench**, versus the original benchmark's ~19% for naive vector RAG and ~79% for full-document-in-context GPT-4. It trades query-time LLM cost and latency for accuracy and full traceability. Best for long, well-structured documents (10-Ks, contracts, textbooks).
2. **MUVERA solves the multi-vector scaling problem.** It converts ColBERT/ColPali multi-vector sets into single Fixed Dimensional Encodings (FDEs) so off-the-shelf MIPS/ANN solvers (DiskANN) can be used. Per Google Research (Dhulipala et al., NeurIPS 2024, arXiv 2405.19504), MUVERA achieves "an average of 10% higher recall with a remarkable 90% reduction in latency across the BEIR datasets" vs PLAID, with FDEs retrieving 5-20× fewer candidates at fixed recall and supporting 32× product-quantization compression. This is the bridge that makes late-interaction retrieval deployable at scale.
3. **HPC-ColPali compresses visual multi-vectors aggressively**: K-means quantization to 1-byte centroid indices (up to 32× storage reduction), attention-guided dynamic pruning (up to 60% less late-interaction compute at <2% nDCG@10 loss), and optional binary encoding for Hamming search. It cut query latency ~50% (120ms→60ms) on ViDoRe and reduced hallucination in a downstream legal-summarization RAG.
4. **ViDoRe v1 is saturated; v2 is the new bar.** Per Macé, Loison & Faysse ("ViDoRe Benchmark V2," arXiv 2505.17166, May 22 2025), V1 was "approaching saturation with top models exceeding 90% nDCG@5" (ColPali originally scored 81.3); on the harder V2 the strongest baseline ColQwen2.5 averages ~57.6 nDCG@5 and the top system reaches only ~61.5% average nDCG@5. ColQwen2/ColQwen2.5 (built on Qwen2.5-VL) are current open multi-vector leaders.
5. **Chunking has become a first-class research area.** Mixture-of-Chunkers (MoC, ACL 2025) uses a granularity-aware router + specialized meta-chunkers that emit chunking regexes, evaluated by new "Boundary Clarity" and "Chunk Stickiness" metrics. Late Chunking (Jina, SIGIR 2025) embeds the full document first, then pools per-chunk to preserve context. S2 Chunking (IIT Patna, arXiv 2501.05485) fuses layout bounding boxes + semantics via spectral clustering on a weighted graph. Contextual Retrieval (Anthropic) prepends LLM-generated context to each chunk.
6. **Graph RAG matured into HippoRAG 2 and dual-graph designs.** HippoRAG 2 (Gutiérrez et al., "From RAG to Memory," arXiv 2502.14802, ICML 2025) uses Personalized PageRank over an LLM-built KG with passage nodes, delivering "a 7% improvement in associative memory tasks over the state-of-the-art embedding model" (NV-Embed-v2) and "substantial improvements of 13.9% in Recall@5 on MuSiQue and 2Wiki." LightRAG (dual-level keyword retrieval) is faster; Microsoft GraphRAG (community summaries) is best for global "sense-making" queries.
7. **Embedding SOTA shifted decisively in early 2026.** New MTEB v2 leaders include Gemini Embedding, Qwen3-Embedding (0.6B/4B/8B, Apache 2.0), Microsoft Harrier-OSS-v1 (270M/0.6B/27B, decoder-only, SOTA on Multilingual MTEB v2, March 2026), and Voyage-4. Matryoshka (truncatable dims) and binary/quantized embeddings are now standard cost levers.
8. **Deterministic lexical search remains essential and is getting smarter.** SPLADE-v3 and decoder-LLM sparse retrievers (Echo-Mistral-SPLADE: BEIR zero-shot nDCG@10 ≈ 55.07, beating ColBERTv2's ~49.95) deliver learned-sparse precision over inverted indexes (Tantivy/Lucene). CSPLADE (Amazon, AACL 2025) scales LSR to Llama-3.1-8B with a <8GB Lucene index vs a 135GB flat dense index. Hybrid dense+sparse fusion via Reciprocal Rank Fusion (RRF, k=60) is the production default.
9. **Agentic RAG and deep research are the frontier.** Self-RAG, Corrective RAG (CRAG), and Adaptive RAG (query-complexity routing) are now formalized in a widely cited taxonomy (Singh et al., arXiv 2501.09136). Newer hierarchical-interface agentic systems (A-RAG, Feb 2026, arXiv 2602.03442) expose keyword/semantic/chunk retrieval tools directly to the agent for +5-13% QA accuracy. Commercial deep-research (OpenAI o3 deep research: GAIA 67.36% pass@1, HLE 26.6%; Gemini Deep Research) define the report-generation pattern OmniRAG should emulate.
10. **Storage stack choice validates OmniRAG.** LanceDB (Lance columnar format on S3, serverless, scales to zero; **$30M Series A announced June 24, 2025, led by Theory Ventures with CRV, Y Combinator, Databricks Ventures, RunwayML, Zero Prime, and Swift**; RaBitQ 1-bit quantization GA at 10B-vector scale), KùzuDB (embedded graph), and Tantivy (Rust inverted index) form a coherent embedded "BYOC" trio that avoids Pinecone/Weaviate dependency.

## Details

### 1. Vectorless / Reasoning-Based Retrieval
PageIndex (VectifyAI; Zhang, Tang et al., Sept 2025) is the reference implementation. It performs two steps: (1) generate a hierarchical TOC tree (nodes with title, node_id, page start/end, summary), and (2) reasoning-based retrieval via tree search, inspired by AlphaGo's search-with-learned-policy. Core claims: no vector DB, no chunking, full explainability/traceability, context-aware retrieval. The repo provides agentic vectorless RAG (OpenAI Agents SDK), a vision-based variant (works on page images, no OCR), Markdown support, and a "PageIndex File System" to scale to millions of documents (file-level tree over a corpus). Self-hosted version uses standard PDF parsing; the cloud API adds enhanced OCR. **Strategic read for OmniRAG**: this is the "reasoning" axis and the source of the 98.7% FinanceBench headline. The key insight — *similarity ≠ relevance; relevance requires reasoning* — applies directly to financial/legal/academic documents the user targets. Tradeoff: per-query LLM cost and latency scale with tree depth, so it should be the *accuracy-tier* path in a router, not the default for high-QPS workloads.

Related: RAPTOR (recursive summarization trees), and the "predefined reasoning vs agentic reasoning" split in the Reasoning Agentic RAG survey (arXiv 2506.10408).

### 2. Deterministic / Lossless / Lexical Search
BM25 over an inverted index remains the precision/exact-match workhorse (function names, tickers, legal citations, defined terms). Tantivy (Rust, used inside LanceDB's full-text search) competes with Lucene. Learned sparse retrieval extends this: SPLADE-v3 (Lassance et al., 2024, "new baselines") uses multiple negatives + ensemble distillation; uniCOIL; and decoder-LLM variants. CSPLADE (Amazon, AACL 2025) scales LSR to Llama-3.1-8B (MS MARCO MRR 41.3, BEIR nDCG@10 55.3) with <8GB Lucene index vs 135GB flat dense index — a major storage win. Echo-Mistral-SPLADE set SOTA BEIR zero-shot at the time. ELSER (Elastic) is a production inference-time sparse encoder. Hybrid fusion uses RRF: score(d) = Σ 1/(k + rank_r(d)), k=60 default; Anthropic weights semantic 0.8 / BM25 0.2 as a starting point. **Strategic read**: the lexical axis is non-negotiable for the user's exact-match needs; SPLADE-v3 over Tantivy gives "Google-style" precision with semantic expansion, and learned-sparse indexes are dramatically smaller than dense flat indexes.

### 3. Vector / Dense / Multi-Vector Retrieval
Current MTEB v2 leaders (early-2026): Gemini Embedding (single 3,072-dim space across text/image/video/audio/PDF, ~67.71 retrieval), Qwen3-Embedding-8B (Apache 2.0), Microsoft Harrier-OSS-v1 (270M/0.6B/27B, decoder-only, SOTA Multilingual MTEB v2, March 2026), Cohere embed-v4 (128K context), Voyage-4 family. **Caveat the user must heed**: MTEB rank ≠ in-domain rank — one production case found the MTEB-#11 model (BGE-large-en-v1.5) won on a legal corpus while top-3 MTEB models ranked 5th/7th/2nd. Always run an in-domain eval set (MRR, nDCG). Late-interaction (ColBERTv2, ColPali, ColQwen) preserves token-level matching; MUVERA (NeurIPS 2024, Dhulipala et al., arXiv 2405.19504) makes it MIPS-searchable via FDEs (10% recall gain, 90% latency cut on BEIR, 5-20× fewer candidates). Matryoshka embeddings allow dimension truncation (e.g., Jina v4 truncatable to 128/256/512/1024/2048). Binary/scalar quantization (LanceDB RaBitQ; ~97% storage cut per Weaviate). MetaEmbed (Sept 2025, arXiv 2509.18095) scales multi-vector at test time via flexible late interaction.

### 4. Multimodal / Visual Document Retrieval (parse-free)
ColPali (ICLR 2025, Faysse et al., arXiv 2407.01449) embeds page *images* into patch multi-vectors and matches with late interaction — bypassing brittle OCR/layout parsing entirely. ColQwen2/2.5 (Qwen2.5-VL backbone) lead the open ViDoRe v2 leaderboard. For when text extraction *is* needed, the 2025-2026 open-source OCR/VLM stack now rivals proprietary LlamaParse: olmOCR (Allen AI, Qwen2.5-VL-7B, Apache 2.0, ~$190 per 1M pages — ~1/32 the cost of proprietary), MinerU2.5, PaddleOCR-VL / PaddleOCR-VL-1.5 (0.9B, OmniDocBench-leading ~94.5), GLM-OCR, Docling (IBM, MIT/Linux Foundation), Granite-Docling, Qwen3-VL. These emit Markdown + LaTeX for equations + HTML tables + structured tags — exactly the user's LaTeX/table/chart/multi-column needs. **Strategic read**: OmniRAG should treat visual retrieval (ColPali+MUVERA+HPC-ColPali) and VLM parsing (olmOCR/PaddleOCR-VL/Docling) as the two complementary multimodal paths, eliminating LlamaParse dependency.

### 5. Chunking
Benchmark hierarchy of strategies (all 2025): MoC (granularity router + regex-emitting meta-chunkers + Boundary Clarity/Chunk Stickiness metrics); Late Chunking (embed-then-pool, preserves cross-chunk context, needs long-context embedder); S2 Chunking (layout+semantic spectral clustering, respects token caps, validated on PubMed/arXiv); Contextual Retrieval (Anthropic, LLM-prepended context — cut top-20 retrieval failures 49% alone / 67% with reranking when combined with contextual BM25 + RRF); plus TopoChunker (topology-aware agentic, 2026) and Mix-of-Granularity (COLING 2025). The user's "QChunker multi-agent debate" concept aligns with the agentic-chunking direction but I found no published QChunker paper — likely an internal coinage; MoC and TopoChunker are the closest peer-reviewed analogues. **Strategic read**: there is no universal best chunker; OmniRAG should route by document type (S2/layout for financial PDFs and papers; late chunking for prose/books; contextual enrichment universally at the heavy-preprocessing tier).

### 6. Graph RAG
HippoRAG 2 (Gutiérrez et al., arXiv 2502.14802, ICML 2025) is the strongest general-purpose KG-RAG: PPR over an LLM-extracted triple graph augmented with passage nodes and recognition-memory filtering; uses Llama-3.3-70B extractor + NV-Embed-v2 retriever; +7% associative memory and +13.9% Recall@5 on MuSiQue/2Wiki vs NV-Embed-v2. LightRAG (dual-level keyword + graph+vector) is the efficiency choice; Microsoft GraphRAG (Local + Global community summaries) excels at corpus-wide sense-making but doesn't directly produce passage retrieval. Newer: LeanRAG (semantic aggregation + tree LCA retrieval), E²GraphRAG (hierarchical tree + k-hop), LinearRAG (linear-scaling), Think-on-Graph 3.0, HugRAG (causal KG). The user's **dual-graph design (SuperRAG layout/structural graph + LightRAG semantic graph)** is well-motivated: one graph captures document layout/structure, the other captures cross-document entities. **Strategic read**: add HippoRAG 2's Personalized PageRank + passage nodes as the multi-hop reasoning layer atop KùzuDB; reserve GraphRAG-style community summaries for "global" analyst questions ("summarize sector risks across all 2025 10-Ks").

### 7. Agentic RAG & Deep Research
Taxonomy (Singh et al., arXiv 2501.09136, v4 April 2026): single-agent, multi-agent, hierarchical, corrective, adaptive, graph-based. Self-RAG (reflection tokens; lowest hallucination rate ~5.8% in a 2025 clinical study), CRAG (lightweight retrieval evaluator → correct/incorrect/ambiguous → web fallback + decompose-recompose), Adaptive RAG (query-complexity classifier routes to no-retrieval / single-step / multi-step). A-RAG (arXiv 2602.03442) exposes keyword/semantic/chunk retrieval tools to the agent (+5-13%). Deep-research systems (OpenAI o3-deep-research: GAIA 67.36% pass@1; HLE 26.6%; Gemini Deep Research on Gemini 3 Pro with file_search over private stores; Perplexity: HLE 21.1%, SimpleQA 93.9%) define the plan→search→synthesize→cite pattern with clarification front-ends. **Strategic read**: OmniRAG's "deep research" surface should implement Adaptive RAG routing (cheap path for simple lookups, agentic tree/graph path for complex multi-hop), CRAG-style self-grading, and a Gemini-API-backed report synthesizer with inline citations and D3.js visualizations.

### 8. Reranking & Compression
PISCO (NAVER Labs, ACL Findings 2025, arXiv 2501.16075): 16× context compression at 0-3% accuracy loss, no pretraining, distillation-only, fine-tunable on one A100 in ~24-48h; +8% over prior compressors; compressed reps can double as reranker inputs (arXiv 2505.15394). OSCAR (query-dependent online soft compression + reranking). Provence (context pruning as sequence labeling). LLMLingua/RECOMP (prompt compression). Rerankers: cross-encoders (ModernBERT-distilled), ColBERT late-interaction rerankers, mxbai-rerank-large-v2, LLM rerankers. Speculative RAG (drafter-verifier) and prompt caching cut latency/cost. **Strategic read**: PISCO at the heavy-preprocessing tier (compress-once, store compressed reps) is a perfect fit for OmniRAG's "optimized storage" thesis — it amortizes compression and shrinks query-time context cost.

### 9. Evaluation & Benchmarks
- **FinanceBench** (Islam et al., 2023): the financial QA standard (150 expert questions / 10-Ks-10-Qs). Naive vector RAG ~19%; full-context GPT-4 ~79%; PageIndex/Mafin 2.5 **98.7%**; specialized LinqAlpha ~97.2%; agentic systems (Dewey + Claude Opus) ~87.3%. Domain-adapted embeddings matter (BAM: +8pp over ada-002). T²-RAGBench (EACL 2026, 23,088 text+table triples) and FinTradeBench are newer financial benchmarks.
- **ViDoRe v1/v2** (visual doc retrieval, nDCG@5); **MTEB/MMTEB v2** (embeddings); **BEIR** (zero-shot retrieval, nDCG@10); **RAGBench** (100k examples, TRACe metrics: utilization/relevance/adherence/completeness); **RAGTruth/FaithBench/FaithJudge** (hallucination/faithfulness); **LongBench** (long-context); **LegalBench-RAG, CRAG, MIRAGE, CRUD-RAG**. Metrics that matter: retrieval recall@k/precision@k/MRR/nDCG; generation faithfulness/citation coverage/hallucination rate; end-to-end correctness, latency, cost.
- **Strategic read**: OmniRAG needs a continuous eval harness (Ragas/ARES-style) with a frozen in-domain golden set per document type; report retrieval + faithfulness + latency + $/query, not just accuracy.

### 10. Storage & Infrastructure
LanceDB: Lance columnar format directly on S3/object storage; serverless, scales to zero, usage-based; SQL + full-text (Tantivy) + vector (IVF-PQ, DiskANN, RaBitQ 1-bit GA at 10B scale); zero-copy; multimodal (data + vectors co-located); used by Harvey, AnythingLLM, Continue.dev; $30M Series A (June 24, 2025, Theory Ventures-led). AWS reference architecture: 1B+ vectors on LanceDB + S3 + Lambda (map-reduce, IVF-PQ, ~200M-vector buckets). KùzuDB: embedded property graph (the graph leg). Tantivy: Rust inverted index (the lexical leg). This "embedded DB harmony" (DuckDB/Kùzu/LanceDB) is an emerging BYOC pattern. **Strategic read**: this trio is the technical foundation that lets OmniRAG avoid Pinecone/Weaviate entirely while supporting true BYOC.

### 11. Novel / Emerging Techniques (2025-2026) worth adding
- **KV-cache reuse / pre-materialization** (e.g., MatKV, arXiv 2512.22195): retrieved chunks are reused across queries; precomputing/storing prefill KV-cache turns repeated document processing into a one-time cost — *directly enabling OmniRAG's "heavy preprocessing once" economics.*
- **Inverse HyDE**: at ingestion, an LLM generates hypothetical questions per chunk; queries match against these. Shifts LLM cost to index time (5× larger index, cheaper/faster/more accurate queries) — again, OmniRAG's thesis.
- **MetaEmbed / test-time multi-vector scaling**; **neurosymbolic retrieval** (VERAFI for finance, arXiv 2512.14744); **episodic/semantic memory systems** for agents; **reasoning-model retrieval** (o3-style) for multi-hop.

## Recommendations

**Stage 1 — Lock the tri-modal core and eval harness (now → 1 month).**
- Implement the router OmniRAG already envisions: (a) **lexical** = Tantivy BM25 + SPLADE-v3; (b) **dense/multi-vector** = a strong open embedder (Qwen3-Embedding or Harrier-OSS-v1) for single-vector + ColQwen/ColPali compressed via **MUVERA FDEs** for late-interaction; (c) **reasoning** = PageIndex-style tree navigation for the accuracy tier. Fuse (a)+(b) with **RRF (k=60)**.
- Stand up a **continuous eval harness** with frozen in-domain golden sets per document type and report recall@k, nDCG, faithfulness, latency, and $/query. *Benchmark threshold that changes the plan*: if PageIndex-tier reasoning doesn't beat hybrid+rerank by ≥5pp answer accuracy on your financial/legal sets, demote it to an optional tier rather than default.

**Stage 2 — Build the "Heavy Preprocessing Once" moat (1 → 3 months).**
This is the monetization wedge. At ingestion, run (and store the artifacts of): VLM parsing (olmOCR/PaddleOCR-VL/Docling, not LlamaParse) → layout-aware S2 chunking + **contextual enrichment** (Anthropic-style, the single highest-ROI step: −49%/−67% retrieval failures) → dual embeddings (single-vector + ColPali multi-vector with HPC-ColPali compression) → PageIndex tree + KùzuDB entity graph (HippoRAG 2 PPR) → **PISCO-compressed passage reps** and optionally **pre-materialized KV-caches / Inverse-HyDE hypothetical questions**. Store everything in LanceDB-on-S3. This makes agentic query-time cheaper, faster, and more accurate — exactly the user's stated goal — and is defensible because competitors bill per-query. *Threshold*: target heavy-preprocessing unit cost ≤ ~$0.01-0.02/page using open VLMs to undercut LlamaParse Agentic (~$0.0125/page) while delivering richer artifacts.

**Stage 3 — Agentic deep-research surface (3 → 5 months).**
Adaptive-RAG routing + CRAG self-grading + A-RAG-style hierarchical retrieval tools exposed to a Gemini-API agent; report synthesis with inline citations and D3.js visualizations in the Next.js/TypeScript/Tailwind frontend.

**Stage 4 — Monetize via serverless BYOC + preprocessing credits (parallel).**
Price on (1) one-time heavy-preprocessing compute (per-page, transparent) and (2) metered query compute, with the index living in the customer's own S3 (BYOC) to win on data residency and egress. Target the **>100M-vector / regulated-industry** segment where Pinecone/Weaviate SaaS economics break down (the ~$300-500/mo and ~100M-vector crossover points).

**Concrete gaps to close in the existing plan:**
1. Add **HippoRAG 2 (PPR + passage nodes)** — the plan has LightRAG/SuperRAG/GraphRAG but not the current multi-hop SOTA.
2. Add **contextual retrieval/embeddings** as a universal preprocessing step (cheap, ~49-67% retrieval-failure reduction).
3. Add **MUVERA** as the explicit bridge making ColPali deployable (the plan lists both but not the integration).
4. Replace/clarify the **Milvus/Qdrant** in OptimisedDocumentExtraction with the LanceDB/Tantivy/KùzuDB stack.
5. Add a **continuous evaluation harness** (RAGBench/TRACe + faithfulness) — currently absent.
6. Add **KV-cache reuse / Inverse-HyDE** to operationalize the "one-time heavy preprocessing" economics.
7. **QChunker** appears to be an internal coinage with no published paper; ground it in MoC/TopoChunker or publish it.

## Caveats
- **Benchmark numbers come from heterogeneous sources and judges.** FinanceBench results in particular vary by LLM judge and setup; the 98.7% PageIndex figure is from VectifyAI's own Mafin 2.5 evaluation and should be independently reproduced before being used in marketing.
- **MTEB leaderboard ≠ your corpus.** Several practitioner reports show large rank inversions in-domain; treat leaderboards as a shortlist only.
- **Some 2026 sources are secondary/aggregator pages** (pricing, MTEB scores, funding). Pinecone's reported sale, Weaviate's rumored Series C, and several pricing figures are explicitly **unconfirmed**; verify against primary vendor pages before relying on them commercially.
- **Vectorless RAG's cost/latency scales with LLM calls** — it is not free; it trades inference cost for accuracy and is best as a router tier, not a universal default.
- **The user's two `ashishpatill/*` repos could not be confirmed accessible**; PageIndex and OptimisedDocumentExtraction were fully reviewed.
- **"QChunker" and possibly "SuperRAG" as named in the plan lack clear dominant published references**; validate these internal terms against the peer-reviewed analogues cited above (MoC/TopoChunker for chunking; HippoRAG 2/LightRAG/GraphRAG for graphs).
